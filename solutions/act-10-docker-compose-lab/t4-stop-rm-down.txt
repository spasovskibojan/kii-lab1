Practical Difference Explanation
stop: Only pauses the action. It shuts down the processes inside the containers but keeps the container instances and the virtual network intact on your disk.

rm: Deletes the container "boxes" themselves. This clears out the individual container instances, though the shared project network still exists.

down: The "nuclear" option. It stops the containers, deletes them, and removes the project networks, leaving your Docker environment exactly as it was before you started.
