Explanation of Lifecycle Differences:
1. After 'docker compose stop': The container processes are halted, but the actual container instances (with their file systems) and the project network REMAIN on the host machine.
2. After 'docker compose down': Everything is REMOVED. The container instances are permanently deleted, and the project network is destroyed, returning the environment to a clean state." > @solutions/act-11-docker-compose-homework/t2-stop-vs-down.txt
